I've always cared deeply about the natural world and our relationship to it, and my outlook around climate change fundamentally shifted after I read **How to Avoid a Climate Disaster**. It reframed addressing climate change as a litany of sacrifices we must endure (that are unlikely to be adopted at scale) to a series of engineering and R&D milestones that are challenging but achievable. And on the other side of solving these problems lies a different, better world than we had before. That belief led me to start [Seren Labs](https://www.serenlabs.io/) and the [Bottlenecks Institute](https://www.bottlenecksinstitute.com/).

I’m also passionate about building up the overall ecosystem of emerging climate & energy solutions, and I do that through my work with [ClimateTech Coffee](https://lu.ma/ClimateCoffeeATX?k=c&period=past) and the Texas Futures Coalition.

You might also be here because you’re new to Austin’s climate scene and I told you that I’d send you a list of things worth checking out. Here’s that list:

- [Austin Climate Innovation](https://lu.ma/austin-climate-innovation) - monthly meetup that brings in local startups to give presentations
- [Clean Energy Office Hours](https://energy.utexas.edu/events/clean-energy-office-hours) - monthly meetup hosted by Michael Webber and John Butler. Lots of UT students and academics but also a general meetup for the local energy scene
- [ClimateTech Coffee](https://lu.ma/ClimateCoffeeATX) - run by yours truly, we bring in local policy experts, researchers, and founders to talk about their work on climate and supporting technologies
- [UT’s Energy Institute](https://energy.utexas.edu/events) - hosts events and conferences affiliated with UT
- [Conspiracy to Save the Planet](https://www.eventbrite.com/e/conspiracy-to-save-the-planet-february-25-2025-tickets-1230661946009?aff=ebdssbdestsearch) - monthly meetup at Lazarus on Airport, skews towards circularity/recycling but all are welcome. They also host a local Signal group as well
- [CleanTX Chapter Happy Hour](https://cleantx.org/upcoming-calendar?view=calendar&month=02-2025) - monthly meetup
- [Austin Sustainability Professionals](https://www.linkedin.com/in/lisahendrickson/) - no dedicated site, but if you follow Lisa Hendrickson on LI you’ll see her post events; they put on a big meetup every quarter or so
- [Austin Climate Hub](https://lu.ma/user/seanvoigt) - don’t know if they have an official page, but if you follow Sean Voigt on LI you’ll see him share events, typically panel discussions focused on energy and/or project finance
- [Solar Austin](https://solaraustin.org/events/) - various meetups, tours, and events
- [Texas Green Building Council](https://www.usgbctexas.org/Calendar) - various events and meetups
- [Austin Hardware Meetup](https://www.meetup.com/austin-hardware-meetup/) - meetup sometimes featuring climate/energy
