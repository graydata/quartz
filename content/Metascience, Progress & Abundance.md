
Michael Nielsen and Kanjun Kiu’s “[A Vision of Metascience](https://scienceplusplus.org/metascience/index.html#)” is my favorite primer of the philosophy, potential opportunities, and open questions in the modern metascience movement.

Here’s a decidedly non-comprehensive “market map” of some of the people doing interesting work in this space:
- Stuart Buck and the Good Science Project
- Eric Gilliam and FreakTakes, amazing work on institutional design and institutional histories
- Matt Clancy on the economics of innovation 
- Renaissance Philanthropy and [their playbooks](https://renaissancephilanthropy.org/playbooks/)
- Kelvin Yu's and Anson Yu's "[Levers for Progress](https://www.leversforprogress.com/)" 
- Sam Arbesman's Overedge Catalog
- Convergent Research and their fantastic Gap Map
- David Lang and the Experiment Foundation
- Homeworld Bio
- Ben Reinhardt and Speculative Technologies  

**Tech Trees**
- Étienne Fortier-Dubois has some [great writing on tech trees](https://etiennefd.substack.com/p/in-defense-of-tech-trees) and recently released a fantastic [Historical Tech Tree](https://www.historicaltechtree.com/) project
- Foresight Institute has backed the development of several [tech trees related to neurotech, AI, molecular machines, and space](https://foresight.org/tech-tree/#:~:text=Tech%20Trees%20%3A%20Building%20a%20tool,can%20help%20advance%20those%20fields.)
- [Tim Hwang](https://timhwang.org/) led a great discussion on tech trees at the 2024 [Bottlenecks Summit](https://bottlenecks.org/) (largely about pros and cons of the form factor)
- The history of [tech trees in video games](https://www.historicalgames.net/digital-dark-ages-tech-trees/)
